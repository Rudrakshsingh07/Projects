Diseases & Conditions
Easy-to-understand answers about diseases and conditions

Diseases starting with G

Gastritis
A condition characterized by inflammation of the lining of the stomach, leading to symptoms such as abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and bloating.
Gastroenteritis
An infection or inflammation of the digestive tract, causing symptoms like diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and fever.
Glaucoma
A group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often caused by increased pressure in the eye, leading to vision loss or blindness if untreated.
Gout
A type of arthritis characterized by sudden and severe pain, swelling, and redness in the joints, often affecting the big toe.
German measles (Rubella)
An infectious viral disease causing a rash and fever, typically affecting children, but can cause serious complications in pregnant women.
Gonorrhea
A sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by the bacterium Neisseria gonorrhoeae, often asymptomatic but can cause symptoms such as pain during urination and unusual discharge.
Genital herpes
An STI caused by the herpes simplex virus (HSV) affecting the genitals, characterized by painful sores, blisters, and itching.
Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
A chronic condition where stomach acid flows back into the esophagus, causing heartburn, chest pain, and difficulty swallowing.
Guillain-Barr√© syndrome
A rare neurological disorder where the body's immune system attacks the nerves, leading to weakness, numbness, and tingling, which can progress to paralysis.
Gangrene
A serious condition where body tissue dies due to a lack of blood supply, often caused by infection or injury, leading to discoloration, pain, and tissue decay.
Glioblastoma
A type of aggressive brain tumor that develops from glial cells, causing symptoms such as headaches, seizures, cognitive decline, and personality changes.
Graves' disease
An autoimmune disorder that causes hyperthyroidism, leading to symptoms like weight loss, rapid heartbeat, anxiety, and protruding eyes.
Gingivitis
An inflammation of the gums, usually caused by poor oral hygiene, resulting in swollen, red, and bleeding gums.
Gastroparesis
A condition where the muscles of the stomach are impaired, leading to delayed emptying of the stomach contents, causing symptoms like nausea, vomiting, bloating, and abdominal pain.
Glomerulonephritis
An inflammation of the glomeruli in the kidneys, often caused by autoimmune diseases or infections, leading to symptoms such as blood in urine, proteinuria, and swelling.
Galactorrhea
A condition where there is spontaneous flow of milk from the breast unrelated to breastfeeding, often due to hormonal imbalances or medications.
Gastroschisis
A birth defect where a baby's intestines protrude through a hole in the abdominal wall, typically to the right of the umbilical cord, requiring surgical repair.
Gilbert's syndrome
A benign liver condition where there is a mild increase in bilirubin levels, often asymptomatic but can cause occasional jaundice and fatigue.
Graft-versus-host disease (GVHD)
A complication of stem cell or bone marrow transplant where donor immune cells attack the recipient's tissues, leading to skin rash, diarrhea, and liver problems.
Gluten sensitivity
An adverse reaction to gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, causing symptoms such as abdominal pain, bloating, diarrhea, and fatigue.
G6PD deficiency
A genetic condition where the body lacks enough glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase enzyme, leading to red blood cell breakdown in response to certain triggers like infections or certain foods or drugs.
Glycogen storage disease
A group of rare genetic disorders where the body is unable to break down glycogen properly, leading to abnormal accumulation of glycogen in tissues, causing symptoms depending on the specific type.
Gastric ulcer
An open sore that develops on the lining of the stomach, often caused by infection with Helicobacter pylori bacteria, leading to abdominal pain, bloating, and nausea.
Glioma
A type of brain tumor that arises from glial cells, causing symptoms such as headaches, seizures, cognitive impairment, and neurological deficits.
Glomerulosclerosis
A condition where the glomeruli in the kidneys become scarred and hardened, impairing kidney function and leading to symptoms like proteinuria, high blood pressure, and swelling.
